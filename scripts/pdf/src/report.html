Singapore is Asia-Pacific’s most strategic wealth hub, supported by strong regulatory clarity, stable policy, and rapid growth of UHNW inflows. Compensation has trended upward due to persistent talent scarcity and aggressive poaching across platforms.

Part 2: AUM Portability Framework & Booking-Centre Mapping

2.1 What is AUM Portability?

AUM (Assets Under Management) portability is the estimated percentage of a banker’s managed assets that can follow them to a new institution. It is a central negotiation metric and typically supported by: client tenure, relationship intensity, product penetration, and historical retention.

Portability Ranges by Seniority

RM Seniority Typical Portability Range Context
Junior RM (0–3 years) 10–30% Lower ownership of relationships; institutional stickiness high
Mid-Career RM (5–10 years) 35–65% Established coverage; portability depends on KYC and service model
Senior RM (10–15 years) 60–85% High client intimacy; stronger personal brand and advice credibility
Market Leader (15+ years) 75–95% Multi-jurisdictional client base; sophisticated portability pathways

Part 4: Revenue & P&L Readiness Checklist

4.1 Revenue Attribution & Book Composition

Book composition verified: portfolio split confirmed across asset classes
Client concentration assessed: largest 5 clients represent <40% of AUM
Recurring revenue confirmed: 70%+ recurring fees target
Cross-asset penetration: percentage of clients with multi-asset exposure
Fee realization validated: fee rate reconciles to real revenue and margins

4.2 Client Retention & Relationship Quality

Tenure documented: 70%+ of book relationships >3 years
Acquisition engines mapped: referral vs prospecting vs network sources
Churn reviewed: <5% annual attrition target
NNM demonstrated: consistent NNM generation and pipeline credibility
Portability assessed: portability % quantified with evidence

4.3 P&L Management Capability

Desk economics understood: RM articulates cost-to-serve and levers
Risk management exposure: knowledge of credit/leverage/FX constraints
Revenue optimization: ability to expand wallet share and defend fees
Pricing discipline: evidence of resisting fee compression
Profitability per client: positive contribution narrative supported by data

Part 5: Candidate Readiness Scorecard & Critical Red Flags

5.1 Critical Red Flags (Deal-Breakers)

Client Base Red Flags

Unverified AUM, single client >50% of book, institutional vs personal ownership mismatch, fee claims not reconciling to revenue, unclear portability path.

Compliance Red Flags

AML/KYC gaps, conduct findings, undisclosed conflicts, client documentation gaps, termination for cause, regulatory record issues.

Behavioral Red Flags

Excessive job hopping without progression, weak client-intimacy language, compensation expectations detached from economics, poor stakeholder management signals.
